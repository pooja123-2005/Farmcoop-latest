Resource Pooling

Resource pooling is a cornerstone of our cooperative model. By combining resources, smallholder farmers can access better quality inputs, reduce costs, and improve overall efficiency. Here's how our resource pooling system works:

Seeds and Fertilizers
Members can contribute to a communal fund for purchasing high-quality seeds and fertilizers in bulk. This allows us to negotiate better prices and ensure consistent quality across the cooperative.
Labor Sharing
During peak seasons, members can participate in labor sharing programs. This helps distribute workload and ensures all farms receive the attention they need, even with limited individual labor resources.
Equipment and Machinery
Expensive farm equipment can be jointly purchased or rented, allowing all members access to modern farming technology that might be out of reach for individual smallholders.
Storage Facilities
By pooling resources,we can invest in better storage facilities,reducing post-harvest losses aand allowing for strategic selling when market prices are most favorable.
Transportation
Shared transportaion resources help reduce the cost of getting products to market and bringing inputs to the farms.
