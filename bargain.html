How Collective Bargaining Works

Strength in Numbers

By joining forces, smallholder farmers can negotiate better prices for inputs such as seeds, fertilizers, and equipment, as well as secure more favorable terms for selling their produce.

Our Process

  1. Form or join a cooperative through FarmCoop
  2. Identify common needs and goals within your cooperative
  3. Use our platform to connect with suppliers and buyers
  4. Leverage our negotiation tools and resources
  5. Secure better deals and improve your farm's profitability

Benefits of Collective Bargaining

  • Lower input costs
  • Higher selling prices for produce
  • Improved market access
  • Stronger voice in the agricultural community
  • Enhanced bargaining skills
